Processing Time To Get Permanent Residency (PR) In Canada

April 10, 2023April 3, 2023 by Gentle Dove

Canada is one of the most popular destinations for immigrants looking to start a new life in a new country. The Canadian government offers various pathways for individuals to obtain permanent residency, including Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs, and Family Sponsorship. However, one of the most common questions asked by those seeking permanent residency in Canada is how long the process will take. In this blog post, we will discuss the processing time for permanent residency in Canada and the factors that can affect the processing time.

Express Entry is a popular pathway for skilled workers to obtain permanent residency in Canada. The processing time for Express Entry can vary depending on a range of factors such as the applicant’s nationality, education, work experience, and language proficiency. According to the Canadian government, most Express Entry applications are processed within six months from the date of submission. However, the processing time can be longer if the application requires additional documentation or if there is a backlog of applications.

Provincial Nominee Program Processing Time

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) is another popular pathway for individuals to obtain permanent residency in Canada. The processing time for the PNP can vary depending on the specific province or territory in which the individual is applying. Generally, the processing time for the PNP can take between six months to one year.

Family Sponsorship Processing Time

Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor their family members for permanent residency in Canada. The processing time for family sponsorship can vary depending on the specific relationship and the country of origin of the sponsored individual. According to the Canadian government, the processing time for spousal sponsorship is currently 12 months, while the processing time for parent and grandparent sponsorship can take up to 20 to 24 months.

Several factors can affect the processing time for permanent residency in Canada, including the complexity of the application, the number of applications received, and the current processing times for each pathway. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has caused delays in processing times for all immigration applications, and the Canadian government has implemented various measures to mitigate the impact of the pandemic on immigration processing times.

In conclusion, the processing time for permanent residency in Canada can vary depending on the specific pathway and the individual’s circumstances. However, most applications are processed within a reasonable timeframe, and the Canadian government is constantly working to improve processing times and reduce wait times for applicants. If you are interested in obtaining permanent residency in Canada, it is essential to research the different pathways available and seek the advice of an immigration lawyer or consultant to ensure that you meet the eligibility requirements and submit a strong application.
